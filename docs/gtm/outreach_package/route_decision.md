# Route Decision

## Recommended Route

Use a three-step route:

1. **Hunter.io enrichment and verification**
   - Use Domain Search for each target domain.
   - Prefer partnership, product, developer relations, ecosystem, founder, and API platform contacts.
   - Verify each selected email before adding it to an outreach list.

2. **Johann approval gate**
   - Review recipient, role, email confidence, company rationale, and exact message.
   - Approve, reject, or request changes before any send.

3. **Controlled sending**
   - Best route: send via Hunter Sequences only after a sending account is connected and Johann approves the final recipient list.
   - Backup route: send manually from Johann's preferred email account using the approved copy.
   - Calendar invites are only created after a positive reply.

## Why This Route Wins

- Hunter.io is strong for finding and verifying emails, but cold outreach still needs founder-level quality control.
- The first campaign is about learning, not volume. A 5-account batch lets us test message-market fit without burning domains or reputation.
- Design partners in this category are technical. Personalization and clear technical context should beat scaled generic emails.
- Calendar invites should happen only after expressed interest, not as a first-touch automation.

## Send Timing

Recommended initial send window:

- Tuesday through Thursday.
- 9:30 AM to 11:30 AM Austin time.
- Avoid Monday catch-up and Friday drop-off unless replying to an active thread.

## What Not To Do Yet

- Do not launch a 25-account sequence immediately.
- Do not use generic "AI payments" messaging.
- Do not claim production-grade verification until the proof-validation roadmap is implemented.
- Do not include rates/Treasury.
- Do not schedule meetings outside Austin working hours unless the invitee requests it.
